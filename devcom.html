Development Communication

"A planned and systematic application of communication resources, channels, approaches and strategies to support the goals of socio-economic, political and cultural development" -Bessette (2006)

Development Communicators

According to one of her publications, Quebral (2012) gave characteristics of who are development communicators. They are someone who:

  • Understands the process of development, the process of communication, and the environment in which these two processes interact.
  • Is not only knowledgeable in communication skills and technique but also proficient in the subject matter to be communicated.
  • Has internalized the values inherent in equity and unfolding of individual potential.
  • Has first-hand knowledge of various kinds of end users of development communication.
  • Has a sense of commitment, the acceptance of individual responsibility for advancing human development.

Diffusion Model

focuses on knowledge transfer leading to behavior change.

Definition of Communication

Information Transfer - Vertical

Definition of Development Communication

Information dissemination via mass media

Problem

Lack of Information

Solution

Information Transfer: knowledge > attitude > practice

Goal

Outcome Oriented: Behavior Change

Social Marketing

Social Marketing is the use of established advertising techniques to promote development goals via media such as TV, radio, newspapers and billboards. Most media-based development projects can be considered as social marketing category, adopting not only forms of marketing but also its tools.

Entertainment Education

A subset of social marketing defined as the process of purposely designing and implement a media message to both entertain and educate to increase knowledge audience about issues and create favorable attitudes and influence behavior. The key characteristic is that the media is not presented in an openly formal education manner; it is presented and meant to be consumed as entertainment.

Participatory Model

focuses on community involvement and dialogue as a catalyst for individual and community empowerment.

Definition of Communication

Information Exchange/Dialogue - Horizontal

Definition of Development Communication

Grassroots participation via group interaction

Problem

Structural inequalities/local knowledge ignored

Solution

Information Exchange/Participation

Goal

Process-oriented: empowerment, equity, community
